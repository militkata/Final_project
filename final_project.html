ABSTRACT

The effect of theta band stimulation on implicit probabilistic sequence learning

The aim of the examined study was to compare implicit probabilistic sequence learning performance under theta band transcranial alternating stimulation (tACS) and sham stimulation (only 30 seconds of stimulation for placebo effects, compared to the ~20 min real stimulation condition) over the prefrontal cortex. Preveious research showed that stronger connectivity in theta band during implicit probabilistic sequence learning is associated with poorer learning performance. 13 university students participated in the experiment, all of them completed both sessions, 1 weak apart form each other. ILearning was measured by the Alternating Serial Reaction Time (ASRT) task. This is a four-choice reaction time task, where stimuli follow a hidden sequence, with four repeating elements embedded into four random elements (e.g. 1r2r3r4r). This alternating pattern results in high frequency and low frequency events, and learning is defined by better performence on the former ones and poorer performence on the letter one. An other variable to measure learning is predictive or anticipatory errors. Based on the literature, I hypothesized that participants show weaker learning performance under the theta band tACS which results in smaller learning scores and less predictive errors. However, I suggest that learning-independent, average performance is not affected by the stimulation. Results did not confirm any of the hypothesises meaning that here I could not evince that theta tACS has any effect on implicit probabilistic sequence learning performance.

required packages: “tidyverse” , “dplyr” , “stats” , “wesanderson”

a trial is a predicitve (or anticipatory) error, if the uncorrect response equals to the very next stimulus, which is highly frequent based on the sequence. not a predictive error: the subject pressed any other button than the correct or the coming stimulus, responded too slowly or the next stimulus is a low frequency trial. this case is not a real anticipation (you can’t learn random events) marking of predictive errors were made in excel, can be checked based on the retained original values.
